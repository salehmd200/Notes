You need to know 1 dose, 2 medication concentration and 3 weight.
***
For dose: recall it, or look it up
For concentration: recall it or see package.
For weight: Ask, or guess 
**Weight**

| Age | Formula | Example |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 0–12 months | (Age in months + 9) ÷ 2 | 6 mo → 7.5 kg |
| 1–5 years | (Age in years × 2) + 8 | 3 yr → 14 kg |
| 6–12 years | (Age in years × 3) + 7 | 8 yr → 31 kg |
***
You get TDD in mg, then in ml (cc), then divide. 
E.g: metronidazole for Giardia for a 10 kg child, conc. 125 mg/5 ml.  dose = 30 mg/kg/day 
- TDD = 30 x 10 = 300 mg/day
- TDD in ml = 300 x 5/125 = 12 ml/day
- This drug is given 1x3x7 = 4ccx3x7
